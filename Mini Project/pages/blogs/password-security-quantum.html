Password Security in the Quantum Age

Posted on March 31, 2025

Quantum computing is no longer sci-fi—it’s coming, and it’s a threat to password security. Algorithms like Shor’s could crack RSA and ECC encryption in minutes, rendering today’s passwords obsolete.

I dug into quantum-resistant cryptography, focusing on lattice-based systems. These rely on math problems quantum computers struggle with. I built a small prototype in Python using the Kyber algorithm—it’s slower than AES but holds up against theoretical quantum attacks.

For passwords, this means longer, more complex keys. I tested a 256-bit lattice key against a simulated quantum brute-force—it lasted orders of magnitude longer than SHA-256. Passphrases with high entropy (e.g., “correct horse battery staple”) also help.

The catch? Quantum tech isn’t here yet, but we need to prepare now. Hybrid systems—mixing old and new crypto—are my next experiment. Password security isn’t dead; it’s evolving.

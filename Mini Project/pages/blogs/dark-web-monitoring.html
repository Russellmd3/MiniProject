The Dark Web: What I Learned Monitoring It

Posted on March 31, 2025

The dark web is a shadowy corner of the internet, and I spent a week monitoring it. Using Tor, I explored marketplaces selling everything from hacked credentials to zero-day exploits.

One trend stood out: stolen data is dirt cheap. A batch of 1,000 credit cards went for $20—supply outstrips demand. I tracked a vendor hawking “fresh” PayPal logins; they rotated stock daily, likely from phishing.

Tor’s onion routing keeps it hidden, bouncing traffic through nodes worldwide. I ran a packet capture—nothing traceable emerged. Anonymity is the dark web’s strength and its shield.

For cybersecurity, this means monitoring leaks matters. Tools like Have I Been Pwned help, but proactive dark web scans (via services or custom scripts) are next-level. It’s a wild west—and a wake-up call.

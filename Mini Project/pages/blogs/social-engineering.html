Social Engineering: The Human Exploit

Posted on March 31, 2025

Social engineering proves the weakest link isn’t tech—it’s us. Hackers exploit trust, not just code, to breach systems.

I ran a simulated phishing test on a mock team. The bait? An email promising a “bonus update” with a shady link. Half clicked it, landing on a fake login page I built. Credential harvesting took seconds.

Defending this is tough. I paired the test with awareness training—teaching red flags like typos or odd URLs. Post-training, click rates dropped to 10%. Tech helps too—email filters caught 80% of my next batch.

It’s a reminder: no firewall stops a gullible click. Social engineering thrives on human nature, so education’s our best patch.

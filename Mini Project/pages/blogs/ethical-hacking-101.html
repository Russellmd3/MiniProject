Ethical Hacking 101: Breaking In to Secure

Posted on March 31, 2025

Ethical hacking is about breaking systems to make them stronger. It’s my favorite part of cybersecurity—legal hacking with a purpose.

I ran a basic pen-test on a mock web server. First, I used Nmap to scan for open ports—22 (SSH) and 80 (HTTP) popped up. Then, I fired up Metasploit to test an old Apache exploit. Within 20 minutes, I had a shell. Weak passwords were the culprit.

The fix? I hardened the server—disabled unused services, enforced 2FA, and patched Apache. A rescan showed no entry points. Tools like Burp Suite also helped me test the web app for XSS flaws.

It’s thrilling but humbling. Every system has a weak spot; finding it before the bad guys do is the goal. Ethical hacking isn’t just skill—it’s a mindset.

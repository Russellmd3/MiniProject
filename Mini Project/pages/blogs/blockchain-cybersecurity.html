Blockchain for Cybersecurity: Hype or Hope?

Posted on March 31, 2025

Blockchain’s buzzed about for cybersecurity—immutable ledgers sound perfect. But does it deliver?

I built a prototype for decentralized authentication. Using Ethereum smart contracts, I stored hashed user creds on-chain. Logging in meant verifying against the blockchain—no central server to hack. It worked, but transaction fees and latency were brutal.

Pros: tampering’s near-impossible; I tried altering a hash—nodes rejected it instantly. Cons: it’s slow, and public chains leak metadata. Private blockchains might fix that, but they’re less secure.

It’s hope, not hype, for niche uses—think audit trails or secure voting. For everyday cybersecurity, it’s overkill. I’m skeptical but intrigued.
